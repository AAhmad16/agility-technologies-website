Approach

Five steps, in this order

The order matters: automation only sticks once the cadence underneath it is stable.

01

Assess

Review current ceremonies, tooling, and reporting against where time is actually being lost.

02

Design the cadence

Set the PI and sprint rhythm, the metrics that matter, and a ceremony structure sized to the program.

03

Run it

Lead delivery directly through the first PIs so the cadence is proven under real dependencies, not theory.

04

Automate the repeatable parts

Build the n8n and AI pipelines that take over status, metrics, and recurring reporting work.

05

Hand off

Leave the team with documentation and a cadence that holds without an outside lead in the room.

About

Founder

Agility Technologies Inc. is an Illinois-based consulting and automation firm founded by Adeel Ahmad, who has spent over 20 years moving between two disciplines that turn out to need the same instincts: telecommunications RF engineering and enterprise Agile delivery.

That career has run through financial services, retail, and federal programs, including delivery work tied to Mastercard, Discover, Truist, Macy's, the U.S. Coast Guard, and T-Mobile. It's the kind of background that treats a sprint board and an RF network the same way: both fail quietly until someone builds in the discipline to catch problems early.

Agility Technologies exists to bring that discipline, and the automation that should come with it, into programs that are still running on manual status updates and ceremonies nobody designed on purpose.
